SRINIVASA RAMANUJAN

The man who knew infinity

LIFE IN A TIMELINE

  • 22nd December 1887

    • Srinivasa Ramanujan was born in the south Indian town of Tamil Nad, named Erode.
    Since a very early age, he had a keen interest in mathematics and had already become a child prodigy

  • Early Age and Education

    • His love for mathematics had grown at a very young age and was mostly self-taught
    He continued to excel in only one subject and kept failing in all others. This resulted in him dropping out of college
    However, he continued to work on his collection of mathematical theorems, ideologies and concepts until he got his final breakthrough

  • In the year 1912

    • he was appointed at the position of clerk in the Madras Post Trust Office, where the manager, S.N. Aiyar encouraged him to reach out to G.H. Hardy, a famous mathematician at the Cambridge University

  • In the year 1913

    • He had sent the famous letter to Hardy, in which he had attached 120 theorems as a sample of his work
    Hardy along with another mathematician at Cambridge, J.E.Littlewood analysed his work and concluded it to be a work of true genius

  • In the year 1920

    • Ramanujan's health had started to deteriorate, after which he decided to move back to India
    After his return, his health further worsened and he died at the age of just 32 years
